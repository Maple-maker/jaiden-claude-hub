Skill 01

/brand-voice — stop sounding generic

Write your voice down once — tone, sentence length, words you use and ban — and save it as a skill Claude reads every time. Generic output is almost always a missing-setup problem, not a model problem.

Once it's in place, every draft starts on-brand instead of starting from zero.

The fixDon't chase the perfect prompt. Give it the rules once and reuse them forever.
Skill 02

/dumbify — simpler spreads

A skill that rewrites anything at an 8th-grade reading level. Simpler language means less mental load, and ideas people grasp instantly are the ones that travel. If a reader has to reread a line, you've already lost them.

Test itRun your last caption through it. Watch the jargon disappear.
Skill 03

/hook-audit — survive the first 2 seconds

Most content dies in the opening beat. This skill scores your hook against a library of proven ones, tells you why they work (attention, curiosity, promise, emotion), then rewrites yours to match.

Better hook in, better reach out.

WhyThe hook decides whether anything else you wrote gets seen.
Skill 04 & 05

/repurpose and /steal-this — leverage

Repurpose turns one idea into a week of posts — carousel, reel script, captions, thread — so you write once and ship everywhere. Steal-this takes a post you admire and extracts its structure as a reusable template you fill in your own voice.

Together they give you volume without burnout: copy patterns, not posts.

Don'tDon't copy someone's wording. Copy the structure and make it yours.
